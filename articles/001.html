What is Hiragana?

Hiragana is one of the most fundamental characters used in Japanese writing.
Its origins date back to the Heian period. Originally, cursive-style Chinese characters (“sōshotai”) were simplified and eventually established as hiragana.

MThe Birth of Hiragana

Hiragana was initially used mainly among women. Many works by female writers of the Heian period, such as Murasaki Shikibu and Sei Shōnagon, contain a lot of hiragana.
At that time, men used kanji and classical Chinese in official documents, but hiragana was used for everyday expressions, poetry (waka), and letters.

Modern Hiragana

Today, hiragana plays an essential role in supporting the grammatical structure of the Japanese language. It is used in particles, okurigana (inflectional endings), children’s books, and many other contexts.
By learning hiragana, reading and writing in Japanese becomes much easier. Try to learn a little every day!
