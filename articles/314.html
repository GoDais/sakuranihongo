Japan’s Beautiful Four Seasons

Japan is famous for having four distinct seasons, called 四季 (shiki): spring, summer, autumn, and winter. Each season has its own unique weather, events, and traditional foods. Many people in Japan enjoy celebrating the changes of the seasons!

Spring (春・はる: Haru)

  • 桜 (さくら / Sakura): Cherry blossoms bloom all over Japan. People enjoy 花見 (はなみ / Hanami), having picnics under the cherry trees.
  • Warm weather starts. Many students begin a new school year in April.
  • Seasonal Food: Strawberry sweets, bamboo shoots, and sakura mochi.

Summer (夏・なつ: Natsu)

  • Hot and humid. Many お祭り (festivals) and 花火大会 (fireworks festivals).
  • People wear 浴衣 (yukata, light kimono) and enjoy traditional games.
  • Seasonal Food: Kakigori (shaved ice), watermelon, cold noodles (そうめん / somen).

Autumn (秋・あき: Aki)

  • Leaves turn red and yellow. Many people go to the mountains to see 紅葉 (こうよう / koyo), the autumn leaves.
  • Comfortable weather and many harvest festivals.
  • Seasonal Food: Sweet potatoes, chestnuts, mushrooms, and persimmons.

Winter (冬・ふゆ: Fuyu)

  • Cold and sometimes snowy, especially in the north.
  • お正月 (New Year) is the biggest holiday. People eat special foods called おせち料理 (osechi ryouri).
  • Seasonal Food: Hot pot (鍋 / nabe), mochi, and mikan (Japanese mandarin orange).

Seasonal Vocabulary

Example Conversation

Talking about the seasons:

A： 日本の春はとてもきれいですね。 (Nihon no haru wa totemo kirei desu ne.)
B： はい、桜がたくさん咲きます。 (Hai, sakura ga takusan sakimasu.)
A： 秋も好きです。紅葉がきれいです。 (Aki mo suki desu. Koyo ga kirei desu.)

*Try talking about your favorite season in Japanese!

Tips for Enjoying the Seasons

  • Check local event calendars for festivals and flower-viewing dates.
  • Try seasonal foods and snacks at convenience stores and restaurants.
  • Take lots of photos! Each season has its own beauty.
  • If you visit in winter, enjoy an outdoor onsen with snow!
